Company Vision
13:30 - 13:45
Josh presents (15 min, then leaves)
Josh sets the strategic frame: where WeTravel is heading as a company over the next 12-18 months. This is not about Q2-3 deliverables (that is Wednesday). This is about direction, market positioning, and what winning looks like.
Goal: Everyone hears the same company-level narrative before writing their individual visions. Prevents island thinking.
Format: Josh presents ~10 min, 5 min Q&A. No slides required -- conversational is fine.
Team Goals
13:45 - 14:00
Nicole + Vish + Ismayil (15 min)
Translate company vision into Team Payments context. What do we need to be true about payments at WeTravel in 18 months? Each presenter takes ~4 min: Nicole on financial experience, Vish on payment integrations, Ismayil on engineering capability.
Goal: Bridge the gap between company direction and individual work. Give the team anchor points for their blog posts.
Format: 3 x ~4 min lightning talks, 3 min transition to writing exercise.
Blog Post Exercise
14:00 - 14:32
Everyone (brief 5 min + write 15 min + share 12 min)
Each person writes a short blog post from October 2027 -- 18 months out. Fictional, aspirational: "What Team Payments achieved and why it mattered." Then each person shares their key narrative in 60-90 seconds.
Why 18 months: Far enough to think beyond the Q2-3 roadmap already planned for Wednesday. Forces aspirational, identity-level thinking.
Sharing: Round-robin, 60-90 sec each. You cluster theme stickies in the FigJam Clustering Zone as people share.
Tool: FigJam board -- each person has a color-coded sticky. Writing happens on their sticky in FigJam.
Synthesize + Prioritize
14:32 - 15:00
Full team (28 min)
Gap analysis (13 min): where do individual visions diverge from each other and from the company direction? What themes appeared that are not on any roadmap? Then dot-vote on the themes that matter most (10 min). Close with a bridge to Wednesday (5 min).
Key question: "What did multiple people write about that we have no plan for?"
Output: 3-5 aspirational themes ranked by team conviction. Feed into Wednesday's planning as a lens, not a task list.

Tuesday Vision vs. Wednesday Roadmap

This session is deliberately aspirational (18-month horizon). Wednesday's PM tracks cover tactical Q2-3 project deep-dives per the existing roadmap alignment doc. The bridge between the two: ranked aspirational themes from Tuesday become a lens for evaluating Wednesday's plans. If the team's aspirations and the roadmap are aligned, great. If not, that gap is the most valuable output of this session.

Moderator Notes

  • Prep Josh in advance: "10 min, conversational, big-picture direction, not Q2 deliverables." He should inspire, not status-update.
  • For team goals (Nicole/Vish/Ismayil): coordinate beforehand so you don't repeat each other. Each covers their domain in ~4 min.
  • Silent writing is sacred. No talking, no "quick questions." Set a visible timer. 15 minutes exactly. Everyone writes on their own sticky in FigJam.
  • During sharing, drag theme keywords into the FigJam Clustering Zone in real-time. Don't debate yet -- just capture and group.
  • Gap analysis prompt: "Look at the clusters. What appeared in multiple posts that is NOT on any roadmap? What surprised you?"
  • Dot voting: 3 FigJam stamps per person on theme clusters. This gives you a ranked list of team convictions.
  • Bridge to Wednesday (final 5 min): read out the top 3-5 themes. Tell the team: "Keep these in mind during tomorrow's project deep-dives."
  • FigJam board is the artifact -- no need to photograph. Share the link after. Themes feed into Thursday's Plenary merge+align session.
  • AI norm: tell the team upfront -- "Write from your own head, not from an AI assistant. This is about YOUR vision, not a generated one."
